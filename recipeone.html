Anda paratha

Anda paratha is a popular Pakistani breakfast dish that consists of a crispy paratha (flatbread) filled with 
    a spiced egg mixture.
     Here's a recipe to make Anda Paratha at home:

    Ingredients:
    
    2 cups all-purpose flour
    1 teaspoon salt
    1 teaspoon sugar
    2 tablespoons vegetable oil
    Water as required
    2 eggs
    1 small onion, finely chopped
    1 green chili, finely chopped
    1/4 teaspoon red chili powder
    1/4 teaspoon turmeric powder
    Salt to taste
    Vegetable oil for frying
    Instructions:
    
    In a mixing bowl, add all-purpose flour, salt, sugar, and vegetable oil. Mix well.
    
    Gradually add water and knead the dough until it becomes smooth and pliable. Cover the dough with a damp cloth and
     let it rest for 20-30 minutes.
    
    In a separate bowl, beat two eggs, add chopped onion, green chili, red chili powder, turmeric powder, and salt. Mix 
    well and set aside.
    
    Divide the dough into equal-sized balls. Take one ball and roll it into a small disc. Place a spoonful of the egg 
    mixture in the center of the disc.
    
    Fold the edges of the dough over the egg mixture to cover it completely, then roll it out into a thin disc again.
    
    Heat a non-stick pan over medium heat. Place the rolled paratha on the pan and cook for a minute or until small 
    bubbles appear on the surface.
    
    Flip the paratha and spread a little vegetable oil over the surface. Cook until both sides are golden brown.
    
    Repeat the same process for the remaining dough and egg mixture.
    
    Serve hot with your favorite chutney or sauce.
    
    Enjoy your delicious Anda paratha!

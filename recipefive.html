Here's a recipe for Muhallabi, a traditional Middle Eastern almond milk pudding:

    Ingredients:
    
    4 cups almond milk
    1/2 cup cornstarch
    1/2 cup sugar
    1 tsp rose water
    1/4 cup slivered almonds, toasted
    Ground cinnamon, for garnish
    Instructions:
    
    In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the almond milk, cornstarch, and sugar until the mixture is smooth and well combined.
    
    Pour the mixture into a medium-sized saucepan and heat it over medium-high heat, stirring constantly.
    
    Continue to cook the mixture, stirring constantly, for 8-10 minutes, or until it thickens and begins to boil.
    
    Remove the pan from the heat and stir in the rose water.
    
    Pour the mixture into 4-6 individual serving dishes and chill them in the refrigerator for at least 2 hours,
     or until the pudding is set and firm.
    
    Before serving, top each serving with a sprinkle of toasted slivered almonds and a pinch of ground cinnamon.
    
    Serve the Muhallabi chilled and enjoy!
    
    Note: You can substitute the almond milk with regular milk or any other non-dairy milk of your choice. 
    You can also adjust the amount of sugar and rose water to your liking.
